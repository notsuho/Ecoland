# Ecoland

[Wiki Link](https://github.com/notsuho/Ecoland/wiki)


Game design and development for climate change news reading and annotation

By Dorina Rajanen

The game design and development is part of a research project at University of Oulu. The project was initiated by Dorina Rajanen (principal investigator) starting in November 2o25. Current team members are Riikka Tumelius (co-PI) and Mikko Rajanen.

The project designs and pilots a serious game to study human-AI collaboration in interpreting climate news. The project aims to collect data on user engagement with climate information and interaction with an AI agent that categorizes news and negotiates meaning with players. Players compare their categorizations with the AI and engage in dialogue when differences occur, contrib- uting to research on climate communication and human-AI collaboration. We examine how users interact with the news to understand communication strategies and framing [1], the individual en- gagement [2,3], and the nature of the human-AI dialogue as well as its outcome or result.

The game concept and the project uses a citizen science approach [4], allowing players to contribute real-world data through gameplay. The AI agent supports both game mechanics and the learning process, enhancing cognitive, emotional, behavioral, and agentic engagement [2,5]. By categorizing news articles, players actively participate in climate communication research.

Tasks in game design and game development with student group (to update):

Brainstorming and ideation (all team/1-2 weeks)
News annotations (1-2 students/2 weeks)
Design and implementation of the game (front-end and back-end design and development) (group/1-2 months);
Playtests: Gameplay sessions with users in controlled environment, data collection of gameplay and AI interactions (group/2 weeks);
Data collection of news categories generated in the game (database) (1-2 students/1-2 weeks)
Deliverables:

Conceptual and digital, functional game prototype;
Functional AI agent as game character;
Functional database;
Research datasets on human-AI interactions,
Gameplay recordings and analyses
